Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Viewing the chart with Age and Smokes selected, it looks like there is some correlation between the two, but not very strong. The plots seem get higher for smokes percentage as the median age per state rises. Utah being the lowest in both median age (30.5) and the percent that smokes (9.7%). Where Maine has the highest median age (44.1), but is middle of the pack for percentage that smoke (19.3%).

Household Income and Obesity seem to have a stronger correlation. When they are selected on the chart, the plots for obese percentage lower as the household income goes up. Mississippi, West Virgina and Arkansas have the three highest obesity percentages and the also the three lowest median household incomes. Maryland, New Jersey, Washington DC, and Alaska have the highest median incomes and are significantly lower in obesity percentage.

Poverty and Lack of Healthcare seem to also have a pretty stong correlation. The plots tend to move up as the percentage in poverty and the pertcentage that lacks healthcare increase. Texas has the highest percentage that lacks healthcare (24.9%) and is in the upper third for percentage in poverty (17.2%). New Hampshire has the lowest percentage in poverty (9.2%) and is in the lower half for percentage that lacks healthcare (11.4%).
